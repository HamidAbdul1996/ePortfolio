Clustering

This unit delved into clustering, specifically K-means clustering, a form of unsupervised learning. While I already have familiarity with the concept, I was able to enhance my understanding of what I already knew. For example, I learned about new distance metrics such as Jaccard distance and the contexts in which you would use them. By expanding my understanding of hyperparameters, I enhance my ability to refine my models and achieve higher accuracies. A more comprehensive grasp of the various parameters allows me to experiment and tune better, which is inherently better for my work (Probst et al, 2019). Clustering is a form of unsupervised learning used to group points into clusters based on similarity or distance criteria without pre-labelled outcomes. Clustering identifies patterns and structures within datasets by organising similar items together making it easier to understand complex data. (Omran et al, 2007).

Artefact

The Unit 5 activity on Jaccard distance can be downloaded here. I will highlight the main features below.

The Jaccard coefficient, also known as the Jaccard similarity or Jaccard index, is a statistic used for gauging the similarity and diversity of sample sets. It measures similarity between finite sample sets, and is defined as the size of the intersection divided by the size of the union of the sample sets. This is applicable for asymmetric data, where we only care for the presence of binary attributes. An asymmetric binary attribute is one in which outcomes are not of equal importance.

Reflections

Reflecting on this task, I found this slightly confusing and I’m not sure if there is a misunderstanding on my part. Firstly, I’m not sure if I was supposed to know that Y &P =1 and N & A =0 but I only realised this from looking at the answer page. Once I had this information, I managed to get the answers following the straightforward formula on the page. The confusion came from ChatGPT which was applying a different formula to calculate the Jaccard coefficient. According to ChatGPT, the coefficient consistently resulted in a value of 1 minus the solution I had reached. After spending some time researching, I think the task may have intended for us to calculate the Jaccard distance, which is the complement of the Jaccard coefficient (1 minus the Jaccard coefficient), aligning with our lecture on calculating distances in clustering. However, the task explicitly requested the Jaccard Coefficient, not the distance.
