Unit 9: Validity and Generalisability in Research

Initial thoughts

This unit on validity and generalisability in research was thought-provoking and introduced new concepts beyond what I had previously encountered. The emphasis on reliability was particularly impactful, as it highlighted the importance of reproducibility in mitigating bias and ensuring robust analyses. Additionally, the exploration of validity underscored the critical distinction between correlation and causation, a nuanced aspect I hadn't fully appreciated before. The discussion of external validity further emphasised that a study can be internally valid yet not necessarily generalisable to other contexts, a key consideration for interpreting research findings.

Reflection

The exercises in this unit effectively demonstrated the value of hypothesis testing, as it's not always intuitive when a statistical significance exists. Learning to use Excel for quick analysis and interpretation will undoubtedly be a valuable asset. While I haven't started my capstone project yet, this unit has sparked my interest in exploring potential statistical associations within my chosen dataset, pending the nature of the data itself.
