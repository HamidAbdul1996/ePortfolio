Exploratory Data Analysis

Initial thoughts

I already had extensive experience in Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA), it is a core aspect of data science and one we have explored in depth in earlier modules. My experience even included working with the auto mpg dataset, a common starting point for beginners. Nonetheless, this served as a valuable reminder of the critical roles that data preprocessing and visualisation play. Data visualisation provides a summary of data, assisting in model selection and informing further analysis stages, as noted by Khanam et al. (2023). Velido et al. (2011) emphasise the importance of visualisation before model development, echoing the sentiment that 'a picture is worth a thousand words.' In an era of complex datasets, the demand for effective interpretation methods is on the rise. Visualisation techniques simplify this complexity, enhancing the efficiency of machine learning applications.

Artefact

The Unit 2 seminar notebook can be seen here. I will highlight the main features below.

The describe function in Python provides an insightful five-point summary for continuous variables, offering a comprehensive overview of the data and facilitating outlier detection. It includes the count, mean, standard deviation, minimum, maximum, and quartiles, making it an effective tool for understanding data distributions

A correlation heatmap serves as an excellent method for visualising the relationships between continuous variables. The use of colour coding effectively highlights the correlations among variables, guiding both model and feature selection with its intuitive display.

Scatterplots offer an excellent means of visualising the relationship between two variables. While I've demonstrated a variation here, scatterplots can effectively illustrate the dynamics between both continuous variables and between a categorical and a continuous variable. They reveal the relationship's nature, and its direction, and by plotting a line, one can also discern the gradient.
