Unit 7: Inferential Statistics and Hypothesis Testing

Initial thoughts

Having covered inferential statistics and hypothesis testing in a previous module on numerical analysis, I felt well-prepared and confident in applying these concepts. This existing knowledge has naturally influenced my inclination towards incorporating inferential statistics into my capstone project, as I gravitate towards methodologies, I have practical experience with. It was also beneficial to go over summary measures and hypothesis testing too, these are methods I have used extensively but it is always helpful to learn about the theory again.

Inferential statistics is a valuable tool, enabling us to draw meaningful conclusions about larger populations from smaller samples. Its ability to predict future outcomes and provide a solid foundation for data-driven decision-making aligns perfectly with the objectives of my project (Alacaci, 2004).

Reflection

This unit served as a valuable refresher on t-tests. While I've encountered t-tests numerous times throughout my studies, I rarely use them outside of academic settings and find them challenging to retain. In coding, a single line often suffices for conducting the test, but understanding the underlying theory remains crucial for selecting the appropriate test. Although I'm proficient in performing t-tests in Python and R, this unit introduced me to the data analysis tools in Excel. Despite knowing of their existence, I hadn't used them before. Recognising their speed and convenience compared to coding, I may incorporate them into my future workflow.
