Unit 6: Quantitative Methods - Descriptive and Inferential Statistics

Initial thoughts

Reflecting on this unit, I realised how much more at ease I am with quantitative methods compared to qualitative ones. My past experiences, both professionally and academically, have solidified my comfort with numerical data and statistical analysis. Descriptive and inferential statistics have been regular tools in my work, and this unit served as a helpful refresher, solidifying these concepts further. It's interesting to note how my mind seems to naturally gravitate towards numbers and patterns. Working with datasets feels intuitive and satisfying, whereas qualitative analysis can sometimes feel less structured. This realisation has influenced my thinking about my capstone project, leaning me towards a purely quantitative approach.

Reflection

The Excel tasks were quite simple for me, given my experience with Excel. The concepts felt straightforward and easy to grasp. However, I recognise a tendency in myself to sometimes stop short at the analysis stage, content with generating numbers without delving deeper into their meaning. Moving forward, I aim to dedicate more effort to interpreting the data and drawing insightful conclusions that go beyond the surface level.
