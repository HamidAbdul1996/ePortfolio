Unit 8: Data Analysis and Visualisation

Initial thoughts

I was comfortable in this module as we have previously completed a module on dashboards and data visualisation. I have used Power BI and Tableau in a previous module, and in my current position use the shiny package in R to create dashboards. It was a useful to reminder to look at the whys and how’s of data visualisation. Good data visualisation is essential because it transforms raw, complex data into a visual format that is easily digestible and understandable. Just as "a picture is worth a thousand words," a well-crafted chart or graph can convey insights and relationships that would be difficult to grasp from tables of numbers alone. By doing the heavy lifting of interpreting the data, visualisation allows us to quickly identify trends, patterns, and outliers that might otherwise go unnoticed. This makes it easier to communicate findings to others, facilitate discussions, and ultimately make informed decisions based on data-driven insights (Eberhard, 2023).

Inferential statistics is a valuable tool, enabling us to draw meaningful conclusions about larger populations from smaller samples. Its ability to predict future outcomes and provide a solid foundation for data-driven decision-making aligns perfectly with the objectives of my project (Alacaci, 2004).

Reflection

Like the previous unit, we revisited the foundational theory of inferential statistics. Since I'm already well-versed in these concepts, this unit served as a valuable refresher. I found the readings on dashboards particularly insightful, as it directly relates to my current job and provides guidance on industry best practices. The creative aspect of data visualisation fascinate me, especially the idea of presenting information in novel ways that can alter our perspectives and understanding.

Reference List

  • Eberhard, K. (2023). The effects of visualization on judgment and decision-making: a systematic literature review. Management Review Quarterly, 73(2), 167-214.
