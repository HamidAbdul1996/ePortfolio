Research Questions, the Literature Review and the Research Proposal

Initial thoughts

Unit 2 served as a valuable refresher on research questions, literature reviews, and research proposals. While I have conducted literature reviews before, the reminder was beneficial. My memory of research proposals was hazy, so this unit was particularly helpful. I've begun exploring the literature on misinformation detection using machine learning and am starting to brainstorm potential research proposals. My goal is to identify an unresearched aspect within this field. As highlighted in the unit, a well-defined research question is crucial as it provides a clear focus and direction for the project, defining the scope, guiding literature selection, and determining the appropriate methodology. Ultimately, a strong research question ensures that the findings are both relevant and meaningful to the field of study (Fandino, 2019).

Literature Review Proposal Outline

Machine learning (ML) has emerged as a promising tool for combating the growing threat of political misinformation on social media, especially with the rise of generative AI. Current research has explored various ML techniques, such as LSTM, CNN, and BERT, to detect misinformation based on linguistic cues, network patterns, and user behavior. However, challenges remain in cross-lingual and cross-cultural detection, feature identification, and model explainability.

Existing studies have demonstrated the potential of ML for identifying misinformation, but further research is needed to address these limitations and develop more robust, real-time detection models. Future directions include exploring more sophisticated ML algorithms, incorporating cross-lingual and cross-cultural features, and developing explainable AI methods to enhance transparency and trust.
