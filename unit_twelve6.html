Unit 12: Project Management and Managing Risk

Reflection

Understanding project management is crucial, and I recognise the need to apply it more effectively during my capstone project. While I'm familiar with methodologies like Agile, I haven't personally implemented them. For now, I plan to use the Waterfall methodology due to its linear, sequential approach, which seems well-suited to my project. However, I'm open to adapting my approach as needed once I begin. I also use Gantt charts for tracking project timelines. Admittedly, I'm a bit nervous about starting my capstone project as I'm still refining my topic. Once this module concludes, I'll have dedicated time to finalize my project concept and proceed with confidence.

I've chosen the Waterfall methodology for several reasons: it aligns with my personal work style due to its well-defined structure with distinct phases. The emphasis on thorough planning and documentation is also appealing, and I believe these aspects are particularly valuable for data science projects. As I have a fixed deadline, I think it works well for suitable requirements (Aina et al, 2022, Mokhtar & Khayyat, 2022)

Reference List

  • Aina, T. S., Akinte, O. O., Awelewa, A. J., & Adelakun, D. O. (2022). Critical evaluation of waterfall project management methodology: A case study of digital management conference project. International Journal of Advanced Multidisciplinary Research and Studies, 2(3), 266-269.
  • Mokhtar, R., & Khayyat, M. (2022). A comparative case study of waterfall and agile management. SAR Journal, 5(1), 52-62.
