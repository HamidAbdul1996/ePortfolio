ePortfolio requirements

All Computing programmes have a key aim to ensure you gain the required employability skills to help you progress in your chosen profession. The e-portfolio is an excellent way of recording your learning, as well as your development of these skills. Also, successful completion of certain modules in your programme will be based on the submission of an e-portfolio. e-Portfolios are a way for you to collect, reflect on, and present computing artefacts and their online learning experiences. You should then be able to represent their entire educational experience from the start to the end of the programme in an organised, integrated and progressive manner (Lorenzo and Ittelson, 2005). The work you present to us in your e-portfolio plays a vital role in helping us assess how well you have met the learning outcomes in the specified module. When you are putting your portfolio together remember that we do not just want to see finished pieces, your successes, or work that shows your command of a technique. We are looking for your e-portfolio to demonstrate your:

  • understanding of the module content and its application in a wider social and global context.
  • ability to draw connections between the module material and the coursework, to achieve the module learning objectives.
  • ability to undertake self-assessment, reflecting on personal strengths and weaknesses, identifying personal achievements, and providing supporting evidence.
We want you to demonstrate your creativity and computing aptitude, as well as provide evidence of your acquired skills as an Essex Graduate. You should therefore consider your e-portfolio as your collection of project work undertaken throughout your programme and it is from this e-portfolio that you make various submissions needed for your modules. Reference: Lorenzo, G. & Ittelson, J. (2005) An Overview of E-Portfolios. Educause Learning Initiative. Available from https://library.educause.edu/resources/2005/1/an-overview-of-eportfolios
