Initial thoughts

This unit provided valuable insights into data collection methods. Although my current proposal uses secondary data, and therefore doesn't directly employ the methods covered, the knowledge gained is still beneficial for future research. Understanding the advantages and disadvantages of each method will undoubtedly aid in selecting appropriate research methodologies for upcoming projects.

Unit 4 Seminar: Case Study on Privacy

The central ethical issue in this case is the privacy rights of the individuals whose property tax records are being accessed. These records contain personally identifiable information (PII) and realising without explicit consent could be a violation of their privacy. There are also issues around data confidentiality, informed consent and scientific integrity. Ricardo should definitely not release the names and addresses if he is not authorised to do so. He should direct Beth to the appropriate department who have authority over this decision. If Riccardo was responsible for determining allowable access to the files he would need to weigh the potential benefits of Beth’s research against the potential harm to individuals’ privacy. He would also need to consider whether Beth has obtained the necessary ethical approvals for her research and whether she has a plan to protect the confidentiality of the data. Beth should not be allowed the contact the individuals involved unless they have explicit consent from the individuals. The department needs to consider the potential harm that could come from unwanted contact. Beth has a few options to progress, she could continue with the study using only the data from those that gave permission, change her research design or abandon the study if its required.

Reflection

The seminar served as a timely reminder of the significance of data protection. It has prompted me to reassess my research proposal, specifically regarding the use of tweets, which can potentially contain personally identifiable information. This has reinforced the importance of anonymising the data I collect. Data privacy and ethical considerations are fundamental to upholding the integrity of research and must be carefully addressed throughout the research process.
