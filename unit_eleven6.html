Unit 11: Going Forward: Professional Development and Your e-Portfolio

Reflection

Maintaining an e-portfolio throughout my studies has been a mixed experience. While completing it, I sometimes feel it's redundant, merely restating what I've learned. However, with time, its value becomes evident. It serves as a helpful refresher, summarising each unit's key takeaways and reinforcing my learning. This is especially useful when revisiting earlier units completed months ago. Additionally, it acts as tangible evidence of my progress, which I can share with potential employers and demonstrates continuous learning.

Moving forward, I plan to continue maintaining an e-portfolio, but with a greater emphasis on showcasing completed projects. Ideally, it would highlight my skills in key data science areas like data visualisation, machine learning, and NLP, demonstrating proficiency in languages like Python, R, and SQL. This focused approach would better represent my capabilities and expertise in the field.

E-portfolios are very helpful in many ways, they can facilitate network opportunities if you share online. They showcase skills and experience better then a CV, it gives a tangible demonstration of your abilities, and it also differentiates you from other candidates (Zhang and Tur, 2022).

Reference List

  • Zhang, P. & Tur, G. (2022). Educational e-Portfolio Overview: Aspiring for the Future by Building on the Past. IAFOR Journal of Education: Studies in Education, 10(3), 51-74.
